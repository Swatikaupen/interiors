India to California:
One continuous practice

My design practice took root in India, where I learned that beautiful spaces are built on layered knowledge — the geometry of a jali screen, the proportions of a Kerala courtyard, the warmth of hand-rubbed wood. These aren't mere aesthetics; they're the accumulated wisdom of generations.

Every project taught me that true luxury is restraint. That every material choice has weight. That light itself is a design element, and the hand of the maker should always be visible.

Moving to the Bay Area, I discovered a parallel language: the same reverence for materials, the same belief that spaces should enhance how we live. Here, minimalism isn't about emptiness — it's about clarity. It's about letting what matters breathe.

My work lives between these worlds. Indian warmth and California light. Ancestral craft and contemporary clarity. In every home, we're creating a sanctuary where heritage feels at home
